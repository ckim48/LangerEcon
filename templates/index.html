About

This study aims to explore the relationship between mental states and financial decision-making. Specifically, the research focuses on understanding how different mental and emotional states can influence the way individuals approach financial choices. By examining how awareness, focus, and emotional regulation impact decision-making processes, this study seeks to uncover ways to enhance financial outcomes, regardless of the size or complexity of the decisions. Through cultivating psychological awareness and maintaining emotional balance, individuals may experience reduced stress, improved clarity, and better judgment in financial scenarios. Previous research in fields such as behavioral finance and cognitive psychology supports the idea that mental states play a significant role in financial behavior. Studies have shown that individuals in positive and stable mental states tend to make more rational decisions and are less influenced by impulsive or emotional biases. With this research, we hope to offer valuable insights into the connection between mental states and financial decision-making, providing participants with strategies for making informed and confident financial choices. This study is being led by Professor Ellen J. Langer, Department of Psychology, Harvard University.

What we will ask you to do

Participants will first complete a 10-minute survey covering basic demographic information (e.g., age and gender) and a series of brief psychological assessments (e.g., mood, anxiety, and stress levels). After completing the survey, participants will move on to a warm-up exercise, which will take approximately 5 minutes. Following the warm-up, participants will engage in the economic decision-making tasks: a virtual shopping game and a gambling game. This section is expected to take about 15 minutes. Finally, participants will complete a post-experiment survey to evaluate their experience with the tasks (games) and reassess their psychological state after the activities. This survey will take approximately 10 minutes to complete. In total, participation is expected to take approximately 45 minutes.

Tasks Preview

Warm-up

Before starting the main tasks, you will complete a quick warm-up activity. This 5-minute cognitive exercise is designed to prepare you for the main tasks. Although it will not be scored, please do your best!


Virtual Shopping

In this task, you’re shopping to furnish your new home. Within a limited budget, your task is to purchase items that match your usual tastes and preferences. For each item, you’ll be given several options along with information about the most popular choice.

Gambling Game

In this game, your goal is to maximize your total earnings by selecting cards from four decks. Some decks offer higher rewards but also carry higher penalties, while others are more balanced. Choose carefully to discover the most profitable strategy over time.
